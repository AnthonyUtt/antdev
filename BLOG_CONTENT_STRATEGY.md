# Blog Content Strategy

## Goal
Share knowledge that builds trust with potential clients (small/local businesses). The blog should feel generous and educational, not salesy.

## Content Categories

### 1. Practical Guides & Tips (~35%)
Actionable content that provides immediate value, even if readers never hire you.

**Sample posts:**
- "A Non-Technical Guide to Choosing Business Software"
- "How to Write a Clear Project Brief (Template Included)"
- "Simple Automations You Can Set Up Today Without Code"
- "Questions to Ask Before Hiring Any Developer"
- "How to Evaluate If a SaaS Tool Is Worth the Monthly Cost"
- "Free Tools Every Small Business Should Know About"
- "How to Audit Your Own Website in 15 Minutes"
- "A Simple Framework for Deciding Build vs. Buy"

### 2. Business Problems & Solutions (~30%)
Posts that help business owners recognize problems and understand solutions.

**Sample posts:**
- "How to Know If Your Business Needs a Website Refresh"
- "The Hidden Costs of Manual Data Entry"
- "5 Signs Your Business Has Outgrown Spreadsheets"
- "What to Do When Your Tools Don't Talk to Each Other"
- "Is Your Website Actually Bringing In Customers?"

### 3. Process & Transparency (~20%)
Behind-the-scenes looks at how I work. Reduces anxiety about hiring a developer.

**Sample posts:**
- "How I Scope and Price a Project"
- "What Happens After You Sign a Contract"
- "How I Handle Scope Creep (And How You Can Too)"
- "Why I Always Start With the Problem, Not the Technology"
- "What Maintenance Actually Looks Like for a Custom App"

### 4. Technical Deep Dives (~15%)
Demonstrates expertise. Personal coding challenges framed accessibly.

**Sample posts:**
- "Why I Chose [X] for [Client Project Type]"
- "How I Built a Notification System That Doesn't Annoy Users"
- "Lessons from Migrating a Legacy System"
- "Designing an Event Bus: How I Think Through Architecture Problems"
- "Building a Plugin System in Rust: A Problem-Solving Walkthrough"

## Technical Post Structure

1. **Lead with the problem** - What was broken? What needed to be built? Why did it matter?
2. **The journey** - Options considered, trade-offs, the "aha" moments
3. **The solution** - High-level explanation accessible to semi-technical readers
4. **Deep dive (optional)** - Code snippets and implementation details for those who want them
5. **What I learned** - Takeaways that apply beyond this specific problem
